Welcome to Sendable, an automated PIPEDA Request generator.

What in the world is PIPEDA?

The Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA), is a federal Canadian privacy law that applies to most private-sector organizations across Canada. With new admendments to the law that have been passed in recent years, Canadian consumers now have more control over their data usage. Under Section 4 of Schedule 1 of PIPEDA, consumers are now able to request access to personal information that a company holds about them, or request the deletion of that stored data altogether. To initiate the process, consumers simply have to provide companies with a written mail or email request to the companies they wish to delete/retrieve their data from. Companies are generally required by law to provide access to the information within 30 days for free or at a minimal cost.

How Does Sendable Work?

To begin the Sendable scan process, you must upload your email data to a Google Drive account. To protect your privacy, Sendable does not require uploads or storage of your data. Once you have uploaded an email ".mbox" file to Google Drive, you can simply log in to Sendable with your Google credentials, and Sendable will (after requesting for permission to access your Drive) search for a file named "ScanData.MBOX". Once the file has been located, Sendable uses the Google Drive API to read the file contents, and uses a keyword search to look for company subscriptions in your email data. After collecting a list of companies associated with your account, our web scraping tool searches the web for company logos, privacy/support email accounts. Once the scraper has collected all the data it can, it begins loading a list of companies that you are subscribed to.

How Do I Get Started?

To begin, navigate to the Google Takeout website to retrieve your email data. In order to get just your email information, click deselect all, and scroll to the "Mail" option, and select which folders you would like to retrieve.

Note: it is recommended to use a folder relevant to company subscriptions such as the in-built "Category Promotions". Using all email data can cause the data retrieval process to be very slow due to Google Drive API limitations, and large files may not load.

Next, select your favourite export delivery method and click the "Export Once" option. Wait for your email data to be delivered in the MBOX format. It could take a few minutes, several hours, or days to arrive depending on how much data has been exported. Once you have exported your emails, download the zip folder you receive and unzip using a tool like Winrar. Rename the MBOX file “ScanData.mbox” and upload it to your Google Drive account. Once this step is complete, log in with your Google account credentials and press the "Begin Scan" button. Note that it may take up to a few minutes to finish this process as it takes the Google Drive API time to extract data from large email data files. Once your scan has completed, the dashboard page will display a list of companies that you are subscribed to. For companies whose information the web crawler was able to retrieve, you will see the name of the company, the company’s logo, the number of times the company has emailed you, an option to either send a retrieval or deletion PIPEDA request, and a link where you can delete your account, if applicable. If any of these are missing, you may see a message indicating that the scan was not able to find the information, and you may see a question mark icon substituted for the company’s logo. You can organize your subscriptions in terms of most spam/least spam using the button at the top of the menu.

Sending PIPEDA Requests

Sendable’s automatic email sender makes it easy to send PIPEDA requests to your subscribed companies. Simply click either “Retrieve” or “Delete” underneath a company’s PIPEDA options, and press “Draft Deletion Email” and/or “Draft Retrieval Email”. You will be redirected to a draft mail which will have the subject line, receivers, and email body auto-filled. Simply click send, or update any details in the email as you wish. It may take up to 30 days for a company to get back to you on their PIPEDA process, and they could require more information from you before they proceed.

Note: It is important that you check the BCC'd email accounts that have been auto-filled. Sendable is currently a beta site and the web scraper may sometimes discover email accounts that are not company support emails. If you accidentally send an email to the wrong department, it may end up in the trash! Sendable is not responsible for any incorrectly addressed mail.

For some companies, a support email may not be provided, but there may be an option to delete your account information instead. If this information is successfully found by the web crawler, a button named “Delete Account” will appear under the subscription options. Clicking this button will redirect you to the company’s deletion policy website. Follow the instructions provided by the company to delete/disable your account.

If you choose not to use our automatic sender button, you can also manually download the email addresses of all companies you have been subscribed to. To do so, click “Download Selected Addresses”, and you will receive a text file containing the email addresses of all companies which you have clicked either “Retrieve” or “Delete” on. You may also wish to not use our email scanner altogether. Please note that we do not store any of the personal data involved in your emails. If you wish to manually submit PIPEDA requests, you may download the email template from the dashboard page, prior to clicking “Begin Scanning”.

Privacy

Sendable is a service designed to make internet privacy simpler. Wouldn't it be ironic if we tried to jeopardize it? We understand that email data is a very sensitive piece of information, that's why we've made sure we never store your email information on our servers. Using the Google Drive API, we are able to remotely access your data and keep it safe where it can be trusted: in your hands. The only information we store about you is your email address. Additionally, the companies that we discover in your inbox are used to build our database of companies that we've already searched for, so that future searches can be quicker and we don't run into issues with scraping a single company's website multiple times per second.
